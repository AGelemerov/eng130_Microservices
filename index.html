Final Project

A project to scan a real world object into the digital 3D world using a simple phone camera. This was done by taking a 360 degree video of an object with a camera mounted at a specific angle. The video was cut up into separate frames and was processed with a disparity checking program to identify differences between frames. These so called "disparity maps" were then combined to produce an object in three dimensional space in software. The common way of doing this is called 3D scanning, but to improve the standard, the old methods were used. Doing this shortened the processing time from hours to minutes depending on the video taken of the object (frames per second and quality).
